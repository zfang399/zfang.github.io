Non-Intrusive Appliance Identification with Appliance-Specific Networks

   

I proposed a novel Non-Instrusive Load Monitoring (NILM) method for load identification and energy disaggregation based on Bayes Nonparametric Learning and lightweight appliance-specific LSTM networks. This work was done during my internship at Argonne National Laboratory.

I have made the following contributions to the project:

  • Designed a simple but effective event pre-processing technique that based on the idea of "velocity" in pose prediction to reduce complexity of network training and make the proposed method robust to multi-appliance scenarios.

  • Proposed to use appliance-specific networks for more accurate load state classification, where each network focuses on one type of appliance and is much easier to train than a huge composite network.

  • Adapted well-established techniques such as Bayes Nonparametric Learning and SMOTE: Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique for more robust unsupervised load state clustering and mitigating class imbalance.

Iris Presentation Attack Detection Based on Photometric Stereo Features

   

We proposed the first presentation attack detection (PAD) algorithm based on three-dimensional features known to us for the detection of textured contact lenses (TCLs). This work was done at Notre Dame Computer Vision Research Lab.

I have made the following contributions to the project:

  • Designed the photometric stereo-based PAD algorithm based on the observation that the shadows are different when real irises and TCLs are illuminated
